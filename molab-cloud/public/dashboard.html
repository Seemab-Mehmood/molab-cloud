How to run a prognosis simulation

STEP 1
Go to New Patient Intake and enter the patient's basic details (de-identified code only), then add at least two serial tumor-volume measurements — day (t) and volume in mm³ from imaging/RECIST readings.
STEP 2
Open Prognosis Simulator, select the patient, and click Apply All Models. The server fits all five growth models to that patient's measurements using RK4 integration and a Nelder–Mead optimizer.
STEP 3
Read the consensus cards: the overall risk band (Low / Moderate / High, based on median doubling time), the single best-fit model (lowest AIC), and the projected volume 90 days out with its range across all five models.
STEP 4
Add more measurements over time as new imaging comes in, re-run the simulation, and download the CSV data, PDF chart, or text report for the patient's chart or MDT discussion. Every output still needs clinician review before any care decision.

What each model implies

Gompertz Growth dV/dt = rV·ln(K/V)

Growth starts fast and steadily decelerates as the tumor approaches a theoretical maximum size (K). Implies the tumor's growth rate is naturally slowing down over time — the classic pattern for many solid tumors and a common choice for tracking treatment response.

Logistic Growth dV/dt = rV(1 − V/K)

Similar deceleration to Gompertz but with a harder, more symmetric ceiling. Implies growth is limited by a fixed capacity — useful when the surrounding tissue or organ space itself constrains how large the tumor can get.

Exponential Growth dV/dt = rV

Constant relative growth rate with no slowdown. Implies unconstrained, early-stage growth — usually the best fit for small, treatment-naïve, fast-doubling lesions where no capacity limit has been reached yet.

von Bertalanffy dV/dt = aV^(2/3) − bV

Balances nutrient/oxygen supply (proportional to surface area) against volumetric loss. Implies growth is limited by how well the tumor is vascularized — how much blood supply reaches its surface relative to its total size.

Power-Law (Guiot) dV/dt = rV^θ

A flexible, general-purpose growth law. When θ is below 1, it implies fractal, vascularization-limited growth similar to von Bertalanffy but with an empirically fitted exponent — useful as a catch-all when a tumor doesn't cleanly follow the other four patterns.

Reading the risk band responsibly

"High / Moderate / Low" reflects the median tumor-doubling time predicted across all five models (under 30 days, 30–90 days, and over 90 days respectively) — it is a modeling heuristic, not a validated clinical score. Always interpret it alongside imaging, histology, and the treating clinician's judgment.
